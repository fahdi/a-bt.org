JUSTIN PRESSLEY

JUSTIN PRESSLEY

PRESIDENT

Resides in Gainesville, Georgia. Justin was injured at 16 from a motorcycle accident that resulted in a broken neck and complete quadriplegia at the C4/5 level. After completing rehabilitation at Shepherd Spinal Center and Warm Springs Rehabilitation Institute, Justin worked has 10 years in the banking industry starting at The Citizens Bank. He has created 2 small businesses, and was an Olympic torch bearer in 1996 Atlanta Centennial Olympics. His community service includes serving as a founding Commissioner of the Georgia Brain and Spinal Injury Trust Fund as it's first Treasurer, and he remains a member of the public policy and finance committees. He volunteered for 10 years as Vice President and Treasurer of Access Hall County, Inc., and cofounded the Access Center for Independent Living. Justin has served on the advisory board of North Georgia Health Systems, and was a founding board member of Our Neighbor, Inc. Justin has been involved in the disability rights movement since the early 90's with Adapt, and the National Organization on Disability, and is currently a Core Leader for Georgia for the National Participant Network.Â Justin is also serving on the Georgia Advisory Committee to the US Civil Rights Commission. Because of a supportive family, he has been able to live independently and as a founder of A-BT, is guided by the Independent Living Philosophy
