Barack Obama signed the Workforce Innovation and Opportunity Act (WIOA)

On July 22, 2014 Barack Obama signed the Workforce Innovation and Opportunity Act (WIOA). The received overwhelming support in the House of Representatives 415-6, and the Senate 415-6. Changes to the Rehabilitation Act and the Independent Living program were included in the bill which includes:

The establishment of the Independent Living Administration in the Administration for Community Living (ACL) within the Department of Health and Human Services (HHS)

Transition will be added as a fifth core service in the mandates of Centers for Independent Living, adding to the original 4 services of: Independent Living Skills Training, Peer Support, Information & Referral, and Self Advocacy Training

Limit young people with disabilities from entering sheltered workshop programs and prohibit individuals age 24 and younger from working jobs that pay less than the federal minimum of $7.25 per hour unless they first try vocational rehabilitation services

Mandates State Vocational Rehabilitation provides “pre-employment transition services” to all students with disabilities

Improves workers with disabilities access to employment, education, and job-driven training and support services.
